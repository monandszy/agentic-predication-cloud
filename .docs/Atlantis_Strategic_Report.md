# FINAL STRATEGIC REPORT: ATLANTIS INTERESTS

## SCENARIO: 12 months - POSITIVE
**TITLE:** Atlantis Navigates Global Disruption and Seizes Emerging Opportunities

### Description
Over the next twelve months, Atlantis will face significant economic headwinds stemming from a global shortage of crucial GPU production capacity, impacting its ICT sector and AI ambitions. This supply chain shock, combined with a projected economic downturn in the Eurozone and a weakened European automotive market, will put pressure on Atlantis's export-oriented industries. Simultaneously, the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, though stabilized by a fragile truce, presents opportunities for Atlantis's heavy industry and defense sectors, particularly given its alliance with Ukraine. The potential for disruptions in fuel supplies and the ever-present threat of hybrid warfare targeting critical infrastructure necessitate a robust national security posture and a focus on resilient supply chains. Atlantis must also proactively address its limited water resources, which could become a more pressing issue under prolonged economic stress.

Looking beyond the immediate twelve months, Atlantis is poised to capitalize on several emerging trends. The global shift towards renewable energy, coupled with a predicted oversupply and price crash of hydrocarbon fuels due to new discoveries, will reshape energy markets. Atlantis's own investments in renewable energy offer a strategic advantage, reducing reliance on volatile fossil fuel markets and creating new export opportunities. The ongoing need for AI infrastructure globally, juxtaposed with the persistent GPU shortage, creates a fertile ground for Atlantis to accelerate its domestic AI chip development and explore alternative supply routes, potentially fostering greater technological self-reliance and positioning it as a leader in AI hardware and software. While risks from EU instability and hybrid warfare persist, Atlantis's strong industrial base, diversified economy, and strategic alliances provide a solid foundation for navigating these challenges and seizing emerging technological and economic opportunities.

### Recommendations
Rec 1: Prioritize the diversification of GPU supply chains and secure critical components to mitigate the impact on the ICT sector and AI infrastructure development.
Rec 2: Bolster national defense capabilities and cyber resilience against potential hybrid threats and information warfare.
Rec 3: Accelerate domestic investment in renewable energy (OZE) to capitalize on global energy market shifts and enhance energy independence.
Rec 4: Invest in and develop advanced computing capabilities, including domestic AI chip development, to address the global GPU shortage and establish leadership in the AI sector.
Rec 5: Strengthen strategic alliances with key partners like the USA, Germany, and Japan for collaborative solutions in technology, security, and economic resilience.
Rec 6: Proactively invest in water resource management and infrastructure to address potential vulnerabilities related to limited drinking water supplies.
Rec 7: Leverage Atlantis's strong industrial base, particularly in heavy industry and chemicals, to support regional reconstruction efforts in Ukraine and explore opportunities in resource processing.
